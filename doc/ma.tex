\section{Background}

STIGQter is an open source tool used to perform RMF and STIG compliance mapping. As an open-source support application, the application is developed to handle publicly released information.

\section{Purpose}

This documentation is meant to satisfy Maintenance (MA) requirements for RMF and detail what pieces of a MA policy may be covered by this application. MA requirements of roles (Section~\ref{sec:roles}) and their responsibilities (Section~\ref{sec:responsibilities}) provide integrity to the application.

\section{Scope}

The MA policy details the security and privacy controls for federal information systems and organizations.

\section{Roles}
\label{sec:roles}

The following role(s) are defined as being applicable to STIGQter execution:
\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textbf{User} -- The user of the software is defined as the individual account given permissions to execute the software.
\end{enumerate}

The following role(s) are defined as being applicable to STIGQter GitHub administration:
\begin{enumerate}
	\item \textbf{Administrator} -- An administrator of the STIGQter GitHub project
\end{enumerate}

\section{Responsibilities}
\label{sec:responsibilities}

Responsibilities are described in the GPL version 3.

\section{Management Commitment}

STIGQter development is committed to occur in an approved open source repository for maximum visibility and distribution.

\section{Coordination}

No coordination with other organizations is required to operate this software.

\section{RMF Control Compliance}

\subsection{MA-4 –- Local Maintenance}

\paragraph{Applicable CCIs:} None

\paragraph{Non-Applicable CCI(s):} CCI-873, CCI-874, CCI-875, CCI-877, CCI-878, CCI-879

The application is designed for exclusively local use, and therefore does not support methods of nonlocal maintenance and diagnostic activities. Any maintenance and diagnostic activities may be performed locally post-installation by the user with an operating system tool or third-party program. The monitoring, authentication, auditing, and termination of nonlocal maintenance sessions is external to this accreditation boundary.

\subsubsection{MA-4(1) -- Auditing and Review}

\paragraph{Applicable CCIs:} None

\paragraph{Non-Applicable CCI(s):} CCI-2885, CCI-2886

The application is designed for exclusively local use, and therefore does not support methods of nonlocal maintenance and diagnostic activities, and therefore does not define audit events or implement audit methods pertaining to nonlocal maintenance and diagnostic sessions at an application level. The auditing of maintenance sessions and the review of audit records is external to this accreditation boundary.

\subsubsection{MA-4(6) -– Cryptographic Protection}

\paragraph{Applicable CCIs:} None

\paragraph{Non-Applicable CCI(s):} CCI-2890, CCI-3123

The application is designed for exclusively local use, and therefore does not support methods of nonlocal maintenance and diagnostic activities, and therefore does not define or implement cryptographic mechanisms to protect the integrity of nonlocal communication. The cryptographic security of nonlocal maintenance and diagnostic sessions is external to this accreditation boundary.

\subsubsection{MA-4(7) -– Remote Disconnect Verification}

\paragraph{Applicable CCIs:} None

\paragraph{Non-Applicable CCI(s):} CCI-2891

The application is designed for exclusively local use, and therefore does not support methods of nonlocal maintenance and diagnostic activities, and therefore does not define or implement remote disconnect verification at the termination of nonlocal maintenance and diagnostic sessions. The secure disconnection of nonlocal maintenance and diagnostic sessions is external to this accreditation boundary.
